1：Glycaemic control and Tarrets

Glycaemic control is a medical term generally used to describe the recommended levels of blood glucose, or blood sugar, for an individual.

People with diabetes may have more individualized goals. Most people with diabetes will work with their healthcare professional to identify the best goals.

2：Low and High Blood Glucose

Potential Causes of Low Blood Glucose (Hypoglycaemia)：

• Taking too much insulin or other diabetes medication

• Doing more physical activity than anticipated,without eating more or adjusting medication

(for example, reducing the amount insulinnormally taken)

• Consuming alcohol • Eating less than anticipated or missing a meal

Potential Causes of High Blood Glucose (Hyperglycaemia)：

• Not taking enough insulin (type 1 diabetes) • Lack of exercise

• Eating large portions of carbohydrate or foods that cause a rapid or considerable rise in blood glucose,without adequate insulin at the time

• Illness, stress, pain, menstruation, all of which release hormones that can increase blood glucose

• Forgetting to take or not taking enough medication to manage blood glucose

• Overtreating an episode of hypoglycaemia

3：Signs and Symptoms of Low Blood Glucose

• Feeling shaky • Being nervous or anxious • Sweating, chills or clamminess

• Irritability or impatience • Confusion • A fast heartbeat • Feeling lightheaded

• Hunger • Nausea • Colour draining from the skin (pallor) • Blurred or impaired vision

• Feeling sleepy, weak or having no energy • Headaches

• Tingling or numbness in thelips, tongue or cheeks

• Coordination problems, or clumsiness

• Restless sleep including nightmares or crying out during sleep

4：Signs and Symptoms of High Blood Glucose

• Frequent urination • Increased thirst • Feeling very hungry – even though eating enough

• Extreme fatigue • Blurry vision • Cuts or bruises that are slow to heal

• Dry skin or increased infections

• For people with type 1 diabetes, weight loss – even though they are eating enough

• And, for people with type 2 diabetes, tingling, pain, or numbness in the hands and or feet

5：Possible Treatments

Low Blood Glucose：If blood glucose is below 70 mg/dl or 3.9 mmol/L, consider taking one of the following to raise blood glucose

• Glucose tablets • 150 – 200 ml of fruit juice • 1 tablespoon of honey

• 75 – 100 ml of a sugary drink (e.g., and energy drink or regular soda)

• 4 to 5 jellybeans or sugary candies

• Wait 15 minutes and then retest blood glucose. If not about 70 mg/dl or 3.9 mmol/L, repeat the process until blood glucose is above 70 mg/dl or 3.9 mmol/L

High Blood Glucose：

• People with type 2 diabetes should drink water and if possible take a walk or try some other form of exercise

• People with type 1 diabetes with blood glucose over 240 mg/dl, or 13.3 mmol/L per litre should frst check their urine for ketones,

if possible before exercising to try to lower their blood glucose. If ketones are present, they should not exercise as this can cause blood glucose levels to go even higher
